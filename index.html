LAYERS OF THE EARTH

Main Layers:

  • Crust
  • Mantle
  • Core

(Ref: NCERT / Geological Survey of India)

Description

The Earth is composed of several concentric layers formed due to differentiation during its early molten stage. Based on chemical composition and physical properties, the Earth is divided into three main layers: Crust, Mantle, and Core. Each layer has distinct characteristics in terms of composition, thickness, temperature, and density.

Proportion of Earth’s Layers (Approximate %)

Layer Percentage (%)
Core 15%
Crust 1%
Mantle 84%

Properties of the Earth’s Layers

1. Crust

  1. Outermost and thinnest layer
  2. Thickness: 5–70 km
  3. Made of rocks, soil, and minerals
  4. Divided into:
    • Continental crust
    • Oceanic crust
  5. Supports life and human activities

Importance of Earth’s Layers

  • Provide minerals and natural resources
  • Control volcanic and seismic activities
  • Maintain Earth’s magnetic field
  • Support life on the planet

2. Mantle

  1. Located below the crust
  2. Thickness: ~2900 km
  3. Composed of semi-molten magma
  4. High temperature and pressure
  5. Source of volcanic lava
  6. Mantle movements cause earthquakes and plate tectonics

3. Core

  1. Innermost layer of the Earth
  2. Thickness: ~3500 km
  3. Composed mainly of iron and nickel (NIFE)
  4. Divided into:
    • Outer core (liquid)
    • Inner core (solid)
  5. Extremely high temperature (up to 6000°C)
  6. Responsible for Earth’s magnetic field
